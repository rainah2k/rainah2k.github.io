Myself in Modern Society

Space to Explore

Living in today’s world gives me so much freedom to figure out who I am. Modern society opens doors, offering endless ways to express myself and try new things.

The Struggle with Authenticity

But with technology and social media, it sometimes feels like I’m performing instead of just being real. I can show my individuality, but it also makes me question—am I really showing me, or just the version people expect?

Finding Balance

For me, the real challenge is balance. I want to take advantage of what modernization offers, but at the same time, I need to stay true to who I really am.

Philosophical Reflections of Myself

Nietzsche

His idea reminds me that I am never a finished product. Who I am today is not final; I can always change, improve, and recreate myself depending on how I want to live.

Mead

I see myself clearly in his theory. My identity grows from the people I interact with daily—my family, friends, classmates, and teachers. I am shaped not only by who I think I am, but also by the social roles I take on.

Cooley

The “looking-glass self” speaks to me because I know I often look at myself through other people’s eyes. Whether it’s praise or criticism, it affects how I view myself. Sometimes it helps, sometimes it holds me back.

Postmodern Views

I can’t deny this. My “digital self” on social media is part of who I am, but not all of me. Online, I can choose what to show and what to hide, but offline is where the real me exists—flawed, growing, and learning.

What do I value most in modern society?

© 2025 Rainah. All rights reserved.

Family

I've always been super close with my family. They're basically my backbone and the reason I am who I am today. They've taught me so much—from how to treat people with kindness to just being a good person. Even when I'm out with friends or on my own, I can feel their lessons sticking with me, like a compass always pointing me in the right direction.

School

School has been so much more than just a place to learn. It's where I found my voice. I remember being a nervous freshman, but through different classes and activities, I started to believe in myself and found out I was a lot stronger than I thought. The friendships I've made here are honestly some of the best things that have happened to me.

Community

Being part of my community has been a huge eye-opener. It's easy to get caught up in your own world, but volunteering and getting involved showed me that we're all connected. It gave me a sense of purpose and taught me to look out for others, not just myself. It’s like being part of a big, supportive team.

Culture

My culture is a massive part of my identity. It's the thing that keeps me grounded and reminds me of where I come from. It’s like a thread that connects me to my roots, no matter how much the world around me changes. It’s what helps me see things from a unique perspective and stay true to myself.
